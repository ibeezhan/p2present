How it works

Three steps to a synced talk page

Everything is just files and one manifest. Host them anywhere — your server, IPFS, or a WebTorrent.

1

Bring slides + video

An HTML deck, a PDF, or an embed — plus the talk recording on YouTube, an MP4, IPFS, or a WebTorrent.

2

Write a p2present.json

List the sources and the timing cues (which slide at which second). The visual Builder writes it for you, with a live preview and schema validation.

3

Share a link

Open it in the player, or send a self-contained link — even one that jumps to an exact moment and slide.

Features

Built for talks, demos, and lectures

One small player that does the things presentation pages always needed.

🔄

Synced bidirectional playback

Scrub the video and the deck follows; click a slide and the video jumps. Forward and backward.

🌐

Any source

YouTube, MP4, IPFS, or WebTorrent — listed in order with automatic fallback if one is down.

🖼️

HTML / PDF / embed decks

Render your own HTML slides, a PDF via pdf.js, or embed Google Slides & the like.

💬

Subtitles

VTT/SRT captions over the whole player or just the video — multiple languages, switchable.

🧩

Layout modes

Split, slides-focus, video-focus, or picture-in-picture — drag the divider, go fullscreen.

🔗

Deep-links & share menu

Copy a link to the whole talk or to this exact moment — slide and timestamp baked in.

Open source

Fork it. Host it. Keep it.

p2present is a tiny, dependency-light static site under the MIT license. There's no server runtime to pay for and nothing to lock you in — clone the repo, drop your content into docs/content/, enable GitHub Pages, and you have your own instance on your own domain.

Hosted & registry

Open core, free forever. Pay only to keep it alive — or to verify it.

The player and the format are free and always will be. The optional paid pieces exist for one reason: making a talk page persist without your laptop seeding it, and letting people trust that a link really is who it claims to be.

📋 Community manifest hosting Coming

Don't want to run a server? Paste a p2present.json and get a shareable link back — a pastebin for manifests.

  • One-click "publish manifest" from the Builder
  • Free tier, no account needed
  • Assets still live wherever you host them (URL / IPFS / WebTorrent)

💾 Paid persistence Coming

Keep a talk online for good without seeding it yourself. Pay once; it stays up.

  • Arweave pay-once by default — permanent storage, one payment
  • Optional ongoing pinning / seedboxes for IPFS & WebTorrent
  • Pluggable backends: arweave · filecoin · pinning · seedbox · s3

✅ Verified registry Roadmap

Human-readable, verifiable talk identities — so a link can prove who published it.

  • ENS-based names like talks.you.eth
  • Gasless resolution via CCIP-Read (offchain, no transaction to look up)
  • EAS attestations vouch that a manifest is authentic
The deal: the format, the player, and self-hosting are open core and free forever. You only ever pay to keep a talk alive beyond your own machine, or to verify its identity in the registry. See the full plan in the ROADMAP.
